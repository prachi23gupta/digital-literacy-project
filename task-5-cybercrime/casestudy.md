### Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick users into sharing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or bank details. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that look very similar to real ones.
In many cases, the user receives a message that seems urgent, such as a warning about account issues or suspicious activity. Because of this urgency, the user may click on the provided link without verifying it. The link then leads to a fake website where the user enters personal details, unknowingly sharing them with the attacker.
Once the attacker has this information, they can misuse it for unauthorized access, financial fraud, or identity theft. This mainly happens when users are not careful or are unaware of such scams.
This type of cybercrime can cause financial loss and privacy issues. It highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links or messages online.
