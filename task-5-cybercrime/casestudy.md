### 🔐 Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. This is usually done using fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

📩 In a typical phishing attempt, the user receives a message that creates a sense of urgency, such as warnings about account suspension or suspicious activity. The message includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks similar to a real one. When the user enters their credentials, the attacker captures that information.

💻 After obtaining the details, the attacker can misuse them to access accounts, perform unauthorized transactions, or steal personal identity. Many users fall victim because they trust the message without verifying its authenticity.

⚠️ Phishing attacks can lead to financial loss, privacy breaches, and account compromise. Awareness and careful online behavior are important to avoid such cybercrimes.
